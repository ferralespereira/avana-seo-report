All changes were applied to: articlemetas.cshtml

1. FIXED — og:url (relative → absolute URL)
   Open Graph URLs must be absolute. The previous value was relative,
   which could cause duplicate content issues in social crawlers.
   <meta property="og:url" content="https://avanaplasticsurgery.com/brazilian-butt-lift-miami" />

2. ADDED — article:tag
   Adds a content tag for the article Open Graph type, improving
   categorization in social platforms.
   <meta property="article:tag" content="Brazilian Butt Lift" />

3. ADDED — og:image with absolute URL
   The Open Graph image URL was present but not absolute. Updated to an absolute URL
   for correct rendering in WhatsApp, Facebook, iMessage, and LinkedIn.
   <meta property="og:image" content="https://avanaplasticsurgery.com/themes/avana/images/services/910x960/brazilian-butt-lift.jpg" />

4. ADDED — og:image:alt, og:image:width, og:image:height
   Image alt text improves accessibility audits and AI-citation signals.
   Width/height allow social platforms to pre-size the preview box
   without having to download the image first.
   <meta property="og:image:alt"    content="Woman showing the results of a Brazilian butt lift at Avana Plastic Surgery Miami." />
   <meta property="og:image:width"  content="910" />
   <meta property="og:image:height" content="960" />
