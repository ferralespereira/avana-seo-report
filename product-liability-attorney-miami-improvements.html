A — Legal-Accuracy Corrections (Fix #1–#10) — the page stated the law more restrictively than it is
1 · Warranty section rewritten to real Florida law FIXED
"Product Warranty Violations" was defined as a failure to warn — the page's own separate defect type, listed twice, and warranty never explained at all.
Now defines express (Fla. Stat. 672.313) and implied warranties — merchantability & fitness (672.314–672.315) — and breach as a claim that can sit alongside negligence and strict liability.
2 · Causation section — added the 50% comparative-fault bar FIXED
Old copy required the product be "used as directed" and that the victim "wasn't aware of its hazard" — neither is the test; a reader slightly off-label would conclude they had no case.
Now states but-for + foreseeable-use causation, that foreseeable misuse does not end a claim, and the material fact the page missed entirely: HB 837 / Fla. Stat. 768.81(6) — more than 50% at fault = no recovery, and 768.81(1)(c) extends the bar to strict liability, products liability and breach of warranty.
3 · "Who can sue" untied from who BOUGHT the product FIXED
"You can sue … if you or anyone you know bought it" — wrong in both directions (an acquaintance's purchase is no claim; an injured non-purchaser was told they had nothing).
The right to claim comes from being injured, not from buying — Florida abolished privity for strict liability and protects foreseeable users and bystanders (West v. Caterpillar Tractor Co., 336 So. 2d 80, Fla. 1976).
